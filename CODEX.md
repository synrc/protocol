Buddha Protocol Non-Dual Meaning
--------------------------------

<p><b>Buddha Protocol</b> generally refers to the foundational ethical
guidelines which meant to cultivate mindfulness, compassion,
and non-exploitation of self and others. It forms the basis of Buddhist
training, focusing on abstaining from killing, stealing, sexual misconduct,
lying, and intoxication to reduce mental stress and conflict.</p>

<h4>Four Noble Truths</h4>

<p>The diagnosis of suffering (dukkha) caused by craving, and the path to liberation.</p>

<h4>Mindfulness & Compassion</h4>

<p>Cultivating awareness and empathy to reduce the suffering of oneself and others.</p>

<h4>The Five Precepts</h4>

<p>Ethical guidelines for daily life: refraining from taking life, stealing,
   sexual misconduct, false speech, and intoxication.</P>

<h4>The Eightfold Path</h4>

<p>The practical guide to moral living, mental training, and wisdom (Right View, Intention, Speech,
   Action, Livehood, Effort, Mindfulness, Concentration).</p>

<h4>Mindful Etiquette</h4>

<p>Simple protocols for visiting temples include dressing modestly, removing shoes,
   and acting respectfully around monastics.</p>
